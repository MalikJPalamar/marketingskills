## LINKEDIN AD CREATIVE

### Campaign 1: Pilot Audit — CEO/COO (Single Image Ads)

#### 5 Headlines

**Headline 1:**
Your AI Implementation Is Probably a Thermostat

*Why it works: The thermostat metaphor is specific and slightly provocative. It forces the reader to ask "is mine?" — which is the internal question that drives a click.*

**Headline 2:**
The Feedback Loop Your AI Architecture Is Missing

*Why it works: Specific gap (feedback loop) is something technical-leaning CEOs and COOs recognize. It names the problem without explaining it — creates curiosity.*

**Headline 3:**
30 Days to a Complete AI Architecture Diagnosis

*Why it works: Concrete timeline + specific deliverable. Addresses the "what do I actually get?" question upfront.*

**Headline 4:**
The Thousand-Day Window Is Open. Are You Building a Turbine?

*Why it works: Combines urgency framing with the turbine metaphor. The specificity of "thousand-day window" suggests a defined argument, not vague urgency.*

**Headline 5:**
Most AI Systems Don't Get Smarter Over Time. Yours Probably Doesn't Either.

*Why it works: Counterintuitive opening that challenges the reader's assumption. Long for a headline — test against a shorter version.*

#### 3 Image/Visual Concepts

**Visual 1: The Thermostat vs Turbine Diagram**

*Concept:*
Split-frame illustration. Left side: a simple thermostat loop diagram (labeled "Your current AI system") — input → model → output → manual correction. Clean, functional, obviously incomplete. Right side: a turbine/closed-loop diagram (labeled "What it should be") — input → model → output → measurement → error → feedback → improved input. The feedback arrows are visually prominent.

*Color palette:* Centaurion dark background, with the thermostat in grey/muted tones and the turbine in accent color (high contrast). No decorative elements — this should look like an architecture diagram, not a marketing graphic.

*Text overlay:* None except the labels on each side. Let the diagram speak.

*CTA button:* "Get a Diagnosis"

---

*Concept:*
Clean, dark background. Large typographic display of the equation:


*Why it works:* The equation visual is distinctive — it stands out in a LinkedIn feed full of people-photos and stock imagery. It immediately signals precision and rigor to the analytical ICP. The "most organizations" line creates identification without naming a specific company.

*CTA button:* "Calculate Your Score"

"Most AI systems do exactly what they're designed to do — and then they stop there.

They respond to inputs. They produce outputs. They do not improve.

That's a thermostat."

---

**[0:08–0:20] — The Problem**

"A thermostat is a closed loop — but a shallow one. It senses temperature, adjusts, and stops. When the conditions change in a way it wasn't designed for, it breaks down or becomes irrelevant.

This is the architecture most organizations have built. Not because they made a bad decision — because this is the default."

That is the Centaurion Method."

## Ad Copy Prohibited Language Reference

Use this as a checklist before finalizing any ad copy.

| Never Use | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Leverage AI | Build closed-loop AI systems |
| AI-powered | Closed-loop / agentic / feedback-driven |
| Transform your business | Build AI that compounds |
| Revolutionize | Architect / engineer / design |
| Cutting-edge | Built on [specific technology] |
| Future-proof | Structurally advantaged |
| Unlock potential | Improve prediction accuracy |
| Seamless | Integrated / coordinated |
| Holistic | Structured / full-stack / architectural |
| Game-changing | [Quantified outcome instead] |
| Excited to share | [Remove — start with the substance] |
| Next-level | Level [X] on the 11 Levels framework |
| Best-in-class | [Specific differentiator instead] |
| Innovative | [Describe the innovation specifically] |

The rule: if a generic AI consulting firm could use the same phrase, rewrite it in Centaurion terms.
