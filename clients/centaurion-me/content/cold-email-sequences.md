# Cold Email Sequences — Centaurion.me

**Client:** Centaurion.me (Malik Palamar)
**Phase:** Foundation (Q1–Q2 2026)
**Voice rules:** No buzzwords. No "leverage AI." No "excited to connect." Lead with the prediction error concept or a systems observation. Short emails. Under 150 words per email unless explicitly noted.
**Sending:** Personalize the first line of every email. Generic mass email will not work for this audience.

---

## Rules for All Sequences

1. **One idea per email.** Do not cram three value propositions into one message.
2. **No attachment on Email 1.** Attachments in cold email land in spam. Attach on Email 2 if warranted.
3. **No "I hope this email finds you well."** It is not specific. It is filler.
4. **No "leverage AI" or "revolutionize" or "transformative."** These words signal that the sender is not a practitioner.
5. **No exclamation points.**
6. **Lead with a specific observation, not a company pitch.**
7. **Short emails convert better than long emails.** 100 words > 200 words for first touch.
8. **Make the ask specific and low-commitment.** "Would a 20-minute call be useful?" not "I'd love to schedule time."
9. **Send day/time:** Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9am recipient local time, typically produces highest open rates.
10. **Volume:** For a high-value advisory practice, quality over quantity. 20 personalized emails outperform 200 generic ones.

## Personalization Guidance for All Sequences

### What to research before sending (5 minutes per prospect):

1. **LinkedIn recent activity:** Has the prospect posted about AI, automation, or digital transformation in the last 30 days? Reference it.
2. **Company news:** Has the company raised funding, hired for AI roles, or announced a tech initiative? This is context.
3. **Assessment results:** If they took the assessment, reference their level explicitly.
4. **Shared connections:** A mutual connection mentioned early in the first email doubles reply rate.

### First-line personalization examples:

**From LinkedIn post:**
"Your post last week about the limits of RAG for enterprise knowledge graphs caught my attention — specifically the point about retrieval quality at scale."

**From company news:**
"I saw [Company] just brought on a Head of AI. That usually means the architecture questions start coming fast."

**From assessment:**
"Your assessment flagged feedback loop integrity as your primary gap — that's the most common finding at Level [X], and it's fixable, but it requires deliberate architectural work."

**From mutual connection:**
"[Mutual] suggested I reach out — she mentioned you've been building out [company]'s AI infrastructure and working through some of the same architectural questions I spend most of my time on."

## Deliverability Notes

### Infrastructure

For cold email at the volume Centaurion requires (20–50 sends/week), use a separate sending domain warmed up over 4–6 weeks.

Recommended: `malik@centaurion-advisory.com` (separate subdomain or domain)

Keep `@centaurion.me` for transactional email and newsletter.

Use Instantly, Lemlist, or Apollo for sending infrastructure with built-in deliverability features.

### Spam triggers to avoid

- "Free" (unless it's a free resource link, which is fine)
- "Guarantee"
- "Limited time"
- "Click here"
- HTML heavy emails — plain text converts and delivers better for cold outreach
- All caps words
- More than 1 link in Email 1

### Domain Warm-Up Protocol

Before sending any cold email:
1. Create the sending address
2. Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records
3. Run domain through a warm-up service (Warmbox.ai or similar) for 3–4 weeks
4. Start with 10 emails/day, scale to 30/day max for a solo practitioner operation
5. Monitor deliverability in Google Postmaster Tools

### Track

- Open rate (target: > 40% for this audience)
- Reply rate (target: > 8%)
- Positive reply rate (target: > 4%)
- Meeting booked rate (target: > 2% of sends)

If open rate is below 30%: subject line problem. If open rate is fine but reply rate is below 5%: body copy problem.

## What Success Looks Like

A cold email sequence for Centaurion is working if, over 90 days:

- 20–30 discovery calls booked from outbound sequences
- 5–8 of those convert to proposal discussions
- 2–3 Pilot Audits closed from cold outbound

At $8.5K–$12.5K per Pilot Audit, 2–3 closures from 90 days of outbound (50–100 emails/week) represents a very strong ROI. The math works because the deal value is high and the message is differentiated.

The enemy of cold email at Centaurion is genericness. One generic email to 100 contacts will produce worse results than 10 specific emails to 10 right contacts. Invest in the personalization.
