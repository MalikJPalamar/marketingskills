## SECTION 2: THE PROBLEM (FRAMING THE THERMOSTAT)

### Section Headline

**Your AI is probably a thermostat. Here's how to tell.**

### Body

You've invested in AI tooling. Your team has run pilots. Maybe you've deployed an LLM-powered workflow or two. Maybe you have someone whose job title includes "AI" now.

But ask yourself three questions:

**1. Is your AI system producing more accurate outputs this month than it was six months ago?**
Not because you retrained it. Because it learned from its own prediction errors.

**2. Can you articulate the feedback loop between your sensing infrastructure and your reasoning layer?**
Not "we collect data and the model uses it." The specific loop. The latency. The signal quality. The error rate.

**3. Do you have anyone whose job is to evolve that loop — not just maintain it?**

If the answer to any of these is "no" or "I don't know," you have a thermostat.

The problem with thermostats is not that they don't work. It is that they are optimized for a specific, fixed set of conditions. The conditions are changing. The thermostat will not adapt.

## SECTION 3: THE CENTAURION METHOD

### Section Headline

**A structured framework for organizations that need to become turbines.**

### Subheadline

Not a consulting deck. Not a maturity model that tells you you're at Level 2. A working system for engineering human-AI augmentation that compounds.

### The Three Laws

Human-AI augmentation works when three conditions hold simultaneously. The Centaurion Method is built on these laws:

**Law 1: Closed-Loop Primacy**
Every AI system must produce a measurable prediction. Every prediction must be compared against reality. The error must feed back into the system. Without this loop, the system is not learning — it is executing.

**Law 2: Human Sensing Superiority**
In any domain where context, judgment, and exception-handling matter, humans are currently superior sensors. The architecture must be designed to place humans at the points of highest sensing value — not to replace them with inferior sensors.

**Law 3: Compounding by Design**
AI systems that don't get better over time don't maintain their value — they degrade relative to the systems around them. Compounding must be designed in, not hoped for.

### The Fitness Equation

Organizational AI fitness is not binary. It is a function:

```
AI Fitness = (Sensing Capability × Reasoning Fidelity × Feedback Loop Integrity) / Implementation Complexity
```

Most organizations optimize the numerator (better models, more data, faster processing) while letting implementation complexity grow unchecked. The denominator eats the gains.

The Pilot Audit measures all four variables. The retainer improves the ratio.

### The Five Sensing Layers

A human-AI augmented organization develops five distinct sensing capabilities over time:
1. **Signal acquisition** — the ability to capture high-fidelity inputs from the environment
2. **Signal filtering** — the ability to distinguish signal from noise at scale
3. **Pattern recognition** — the ability to identify structure in historical data streams
4. **Contextual interpretation** — the ability to apply judgment to ambiguous signals (where humans remain superior)
5. **Predictive synthesis** — the ability to combine layers 1–4 into accurate forward-looking models

Most organizations have developed layers 1–2. They are working on layer 3. Layers 4–5 are where competitive advantage is built.

## COPY PRINCIPLES FOR ONGOING ITERATIONS

When editing or expanding this copy, maintain these rules:

1. **No platform names in place of description.** Don't say "AI-powered" — say what the system specifically does.
2. **The turbine metaphor is the anchor.** It is specific, visual, and technically accurate. Use it, don't abandon it for something fresher.
3. **Loss aversion is structural, not fearful.** The Thousand-Day Window argument is about competitive structure, not existential risk. Keep it precise.
4. **Every section earns the next.** The hero earns the problem section. The problem earns the method. The method earns the engagement offer. Don't reorganize without understanding the logical sequence.
5. **Quantify wherever possible.** "Most AI implementations" is weaker than "organizations at Level 1–3 on the 11 Levels framework." Use the framework as precision language.
6. **Malik speaks.** The tone throughout is first-person or direct-address. Not corporate third-person. "Centaurion.me exists to build that architecture" — not "Our team specializes in building architecture."
