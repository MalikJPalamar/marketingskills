## LinkedIn Content Strategy

### Why LinkedIn Is the Primary Channel

Centaurion's ICP — CEOs, COOs, CTOs, VP Engineering at mid-market organizations — is more reachable on LinkedIn than any other platform. They publish there, consume content there, and make professional decisions there. Malik's voice (systems engineer who reads philosophy) is rare enough on LinkedIn to stand out significantly in the AI consulting noise.

The goal is not virality. The goal is to become the reference point in a specific person's mind when they think "I need to figure out our AI architecture." That requires consistency over time, not occasional viral posts.

### LinkedIn Content Formats

#### Format 1: Long-Form Insight Post (800–1,200 words)

**Frequency:** 1x per week, Thursday or Friday
**Purpose:** Establish Malik as a systems thinker, not a trend-chaser
**Structure:**
- Open with a counterintuitive observation or specific claim
- Develop the argument with 3–4 supporting points
- Ground it in the Centaurion framework (Three Laws, 11 Levels, Fitness Equation)
- End with a question or a direct challenge to conventional wisdom

**Example topics:**
- "Why Your AI Implementation Is Getting Worse Over Time (Even Though It Looks Like It's Working)"
- "The Thermostat vs Turbine Problem in Human-AI Augmentation"
- "Most AI Governance Frameworks Are Missing the Feedback Loop Entirely"
- "What 'Prediction Error' Has to Do With Your Organization's AI Fitness"

**Format note:** LinkedIn long-form works best with short paragraphs (1–2 sentences), line breaks between every 3–4 sentences, and a hook in the first 2 lines that forces the reader to click "see more."

#### Format 2: Short Insight (150–300 words)

**Frequency:** 2x per week, Tuesday and Wednesday
**Purpose:** Demonstrate precision of thinking; seed framework vocabulary into the feed
**Structure:**
- One specific claim or observation
- 3–5 sentences of explanation
- Optional: "Thread coming on this Thursday" to drive anticipation

**Example posts:**

```
Post A:
Most AI implementations aren't failing because the model is wrong.
They're failing because the feedback loop isn't closed.

Without a loop from output → reality check → error measurement → model update, 
you're not building an AI system. You're deploying a fixed-function tool.

The name for this in the Centaurion Method is Closed-Loop Primacy — 
the first law of human-AI augmentation.

It is the single most common gap I see in the 30-Day Pilot Audits.
```

```
Post B:
The Fitness Equation for an AI-augmented organization:

Fitness = (Sensing × Reasoning × Feedback) / Implementation Complexity

Most organizations are maximizing the numerator.
None are managing the denominator.

Implementation complexity is the silent killer of AI ROI.
```

```
Post C:
A question I ask every organization I work with:

"When your AI system makes a prediction that's wrong, what happens next?"

The most common answer: "We correct it manually."

The correct answer: "We log the error, measure it, and the system adjusts for next time."

If the answer is the first one, you have a thermostat.
```

#### Format 3: Framework Visual / Graphic Post

**Frequency:** 1x per week, Monday
**Purpose:** Visual anchoring of Centaurion IP; shareable, citation-worthy
**Formats:**
- Infographic of the 11 Levels (progression view)
- Diagram of the Five Sensing Layers
- Fitness Equation visual with example organization scores
- "Thermostat vs Turbine" comparison graphic

**Production approach for Phase 1:** Use Canva or Figma with a consistent visual system (Centaurion brand colors, clean typography, no stock photos). The engineering audience will respect functional diagrams over slick marketing visuals.

**Example concepts:**
- "The 11 Levels of Agentic Engineering — Where Does Your Org Sit?" (horizontal progression bar with level descriptions)
- "Five Sensing Layers — Building from Signal Acquisition to Predictive Synthesis"
- "Closed-Loop AI System Diagram" (simple architecture diagram — Nova → Cortex → action → measurement → feedback)

#### Format 4: Case Study Snippet / Transformation Story

**Frequency:** 1x every 2 weeks
**Purpose:** Social proof, demonstrating methodology in action
**Structure:**
- "The situation: [brief, anonymized client context]"
- "The diagnosis: [what the Pilot Audit found in 11 Levels terms]"
- "The change: [specific architectural shift implemented in retainer]"
- "The result: [quantified outcome, even if partial — percentages, time savings, accuracy improvements]"

**Phase 1 note:** Until formal case studies exist, use hypothetical illustrations based on real patterns Malik has observed: "I see this pattern repeatedly in mid-market organizations. Here's what it looks like and what changes it."

#### Format 5: Prediction Error of the Week

**Frequency:** 1x per week, Friday morning
**Purpose:** Signal quality over time; demonstrate Malik's analytical framework in action
**Concept:** Each Friday, share a short post (150–250 words) that identifies a specific prediction the AI/tech industry made, measures the error, and draws a systems lesson.

**Format template:**
```
Prediction Error of the Week

The prediction: [What was claimed, by whom, when]
What happened: [The actual outcome]
Error magnitude: [Qualitative or quantitative gap]
What this tells us about [sensing / feedback loops / organizational readiness]:
[2–3 sentences of analysis]

—

This is a recurring series. The reason I run it is not to point fingers at bad predictions. 
It's because prediction error is the most honest measure of systems intelligence.

If you're not measuring your own prediction errors, you're not learning.
```

**Example topics:**
- "GPT-4 will replace most knowledge workers by 2024" — the prediction error analysis
- "AI chips will democratize by 2025" — where the supply chain assumptions failed
- "RAG will solve hallucination" — what the prediction got right and wrong
- Corporate AI transformation timelines from 2023 McKinsey reports — measured against 2026 reality

## X/Twitter Content Strategy

### The Audience Difference

X/Twitter is where developers, ML engineers, AI researchers, and builders live. This is not Malik's primary sales channel — it is his technical credibility channel. Selling to Segment A (technical leaders) requires that they trust Malik's technical depth. X/Twitter is where you build that trust before LinkedIn brings the business conversation.

### Content Approach for X/Twitter

**Thread format for 11 Levels:**
Break the 11 Levels of Agentic Engineering into a Twitter thread that can be consumed in 5 minutes. Each level gets 2–3 tweets: the definition, the engineering indicators, and the transition requirements to the next level.

```
Thread: The 11 Levels of Agentic Engineering

Most orgs think they're at Level 6. 
Most are at Level 3.

Here's the full framework (thread):

/1 Level 1: Rule-Based Automation
Your system follows explicit IF-THEN rules. There is no learning. No adaptation. It executes exactly what it was programmed to execute.

Indicators: Scripted workflows, no stochastic output, deterministic behavior
Gap to Level 2: Introduce probabilistic output + basic state tracking

/2 Level 2: Reactive Agent
Your system can respond to environmental inputs using pre-trained models. It reacts but does not remember. No persistent state between sessions.

... [continue for all 11 levels]

/12 What level is your org at?

The free assessment at centaurion.me takes 5 minutes.
Most people are surprised by the answer.
```

**Short technical observations:**
- 280-character technical claims that demonstrate systems thinking
- Responses to major AI releases with a "what this means for organizational agentic capability" frame
- Engagement with AI researchers and engineers — direct technical discussion, not promotional posts

**Frequency:** 3–5 tweets/day, including replies and reposts. On X/Twitter, engagement requires volume and responsiveness, not just publishing.

**Topics mix:**
- 40% original thoughts on agentic architecture, feedback loops, systems design
- 30% responses and discussions with technical community
- 20% cross-posts (excerpts) from LinkedIn long-form
- 10% 11 Levels framework promotion

## GitHub Content Strategy

### The GitHub README as Marketing

The `MalikJPalamar/centaurion-agentic-engineering-lvl` repository is both a technical artifact and a marketing asset. Engineers and technical leaders who discover the 11 Levels framework on GitHub will evaluate Malik's credibility based on the quality and rigor of the documentation.

### GitHub Content Actions

**Bi-weekly:** Review and update the main README.md to:
- Add new real-world examples for each level
- Add references to recent implementations or research that validate the framework
- Add a clear "How to Use This Framework in Your Organization" section with actionable steps
- Link to centaurion.me for the interactive assessment
- Maintain a CHANGELOG.md that shows the framework is actively evolving

**Monthly:** Write a technical blog post or discussion post in the GitHub Discussions section on:
- A specific pattern observed at one of the 11 Levels
- An architectural challenge in building closed-loop agentic systems
- A comparison of different approaches to building sensing layers

**Quarterly:** Release a new version of the framework with updated level definitions, new supporting tools, or additional resources.

The GitHub profile README is an often-neglected asset. For Malik's profile:
- Brief bio linking to centaurion.me and The Prediction Error Brief
- Pinned repositories: 11 Levels, any other infrastructure tools he's built
- Short description of active projects (Nova, Cortex, Centaurion infrastructure)
- A callout: "If you're building closed-loop agentic systems and want to compare notes, email [address]"

## Cross-Posting Rules

1. **Long-form LinkedIn posts → do not post verbatim on X/Twitter.** Instead, extract the most interesting claim or contrarian point and post it as a standalone tweet with a link.

2. **X/Twitter threads → repurpose as LinkedIn carousels or visual posts**, not text. The format mismatch is too significant to cross-post directly.

3. **GitHub technical posts → link from X/Twitter, do not cross-post to LinkedIn** unless the content has clear executive relevance.

4. **LinkedIn posts → do not post to GitHub.** Separate audiences require separate framings.

5. **Newsletter content → can be excerpted in LinkedIn posts** after the newsletter sends (24-hour delay to reward subscribers first).

## Engagement Strategy

### On LinkedIn

- Respond to every comment within 24 hours during Phase 1
- Ask follow-up questions in comment responses — this extends reach
- Engage with 3–5 ICP-relevant posts per day (meaningful comments, not "great post!")
- Follow and engage with content from: future referral partners, potential ICP contacts, adjacent thought leaders in agentic AI

### On X/Twitter

- Respond to replies on technical threads — the conversation is the content
- Engage with AI researchers, engineers building agentic systems, and practitioners
- Repost (with added commentary) relevant technical posts from the research community

### Tracking Engagement Quality

Weekly: screenshot or log the 3 posts with highest reach and the 3 posts that generated the most conversation. Use this to calibrate which topics resonate with the ICP vs. the general AI-curious audience.

The goal metric is not total impressions. It is: "How many people in my ICP saw this content this week?"
