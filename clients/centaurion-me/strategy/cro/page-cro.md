# Page CRO — Centaurion.me
*April 2026 · Skill: page-cro · Author: Malik Palamar*

---

## CRO Philosophy for Centaurion

Centaurion's buyers are high-trust, slow-moving, and analytically rigorous. Standard CRO tactics (countdown timers, fake scarcity, benefit-stacking bullets) will backfire. The conversion goal for most pages is not a purchase — it is a **micro-commitment** (email signup, assessment, discovery call booking). Every optimization should reduce friction on that specific next step.

---

## Homepage CRO

### Current Goal: Discovery Call Booking or Email Signup

**Above the Fold**

| Element | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| H1 | Lead with the consequence, not the feature: "The window for AI positioning closes in 2027." — not "Human-AI Augmentation Advisory" |
| Subheadline | One sentence on what Centaurion does and why it's different: "Centaurion is the methodology for getting it right. Grounded in science. Built in production." |
| Primary CTA | One CTA: "Book a Discovery Call" — NOT "Learn More" or "Explore Our Services" |
| Secondary CTA | "Take the Assessment" (lower commitment) |
| Hero visual | Avoid stock photos. Use an architecture diagram (exo-cortex stack, fitness equation, or 11 Levels visual). |

**Social Proof Section**

Place immediately below the hero — not below the fold:
- AOB case study metrics (3 key numbers from the pilot)
- GitHub repo star count (social proof for technical visitors)
- "Built by a practitioner, not a theorist" — with link to the exo-cortex documentation

**Three-Section Structure (below social proof)**

Each section addresses one ICP:
1. "For organizations navigating AI transformation" → links to `/work-with-us`
2. "For technical leaders building agentic systems" → links to `/agentic-engineering`
3. "For practitioners building your own exo-cortex" → links to `/methodology`

**Footer CTA**

"The methodology is published. Implementation is what we offer. → Book a Discovery Call"

### Conversion Killers to Fix

- [ ] No "About" above the fold — Malik's name and credibility must appear in the first screen
- [ ] Don't list services — list outcomes ("Your organization will have a closed-loop AI architecture, not just a roadmap")
- [ ] Don't use the word "synergy", "leverage", or "AI-powered" anywhere

## Work With Us CRO (`/work-with-us`)

### Goal: Discovery call booking

**Above the fold**:
- What the engagement delivers (not what Centaurion is)
- Three engagement types with "starting from" price for Tier 1 only
- Primary CTA: "Book a 30-Minute Discovery Call" with calendar embed (Calendly or Cal.com)

**Trust signals**:
- AOB pilot deliverables shown visually (dashboard screenshot, workbook preview)
- "3–4 engagements per quarter" scarcity signal — authentic, not manufactured
- "The methodology is published — we have nothing to hide" trust statement

**Form optimization** (if contact form rather than calendar):
- Maximum 3 fields: Name, Email, "Describe your AI situation in one sentence"
- No phone number field
- No "How did you hear about us?" — adds friction, track via UTM instead

## Assessment Tool CRO (`/agentic-engineering/assessment`)

### Goal: Email capture (40%+ rate)

**Pre-assessment page**:
- Headline: "What Agentic Engineering Level Are You?"
- 3 bullet points on what they'll learn
- CTA: "Take the Assessment (10 questions, 3 minutes)"
- No email required to start

**Results page**:
- Show level immediately (no gate)
- Email gate for full report: "Enter your email for your complete Level [N] analysis and roadmap"
- Value framing: "Your personalized gap analysis + the 3 highest-leverage actions to reach Level [N+1]"

**Optimization levers**:
- Test: immediate level reveal vs. "enter email to see your level"
- Test: "Your full report" vs. "Your personalized roadmap"
- Test: One-field (email only) vs. two-field (name + email)

## Blog Post CRO

### Goal: Email signup

Every blog post needs:
1. **Inline CTA** mid-article (around 50% scroll depth): "Get weekly prediction error insights → [Email]"
2. **End-of-post CTA**: "If this resonated, the weekly brief goes deeper. → Subscribe"
3. **Related content links**: 2–3 internal links to methodology or case study pages

**What not to do**:
- No popup on blog posts (conflicts with the "no hype" brand voice)
- No "Click here to learn more about our services" CTAs — too salesy for the ICP
- No social share buttons (low ROI for this audience, adds visual noise)

## Conversion Rate Benchmarks (Advisory Site)

| Page | Target CVR | Metric |
|---|---|---|
| Homepage → Discovery call | 1–3% of visitors | Calendly bookings / unique visitors |
| Methodology hub → Email | 3–8% | Email signups / unique visitors |
| Assessment → Email capture | 35–50% | Emails captured / assessments completed |
| Blog post → Email | 1–3% | Email signups / unique blog visitors |
| Discovery call → Pilot proposal | 40–60% | Proposals sent / calls completed |
| Pilot proposal → Signed | 50–70% | Signed engagements / proposals sent |

*At Phase 1 traffic levels, use qualitative methods (5-second test, user interviews) rather than statistical A/B testing.*
