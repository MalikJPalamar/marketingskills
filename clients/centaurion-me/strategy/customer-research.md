## Persona A: The Transformation-Responsible Executive

### Profile
- **Titles**: CEO, COO, CTO, Chief Digital Officer, VP of Operations
- **Company size**: $5M–$50M revenue, 20–250 employees
- **Industry**: Professional services, wellness, healthcare-adjacent, specialty manufacturing, mission-driven orgs
- **Reports to**: Board / founder
- **AI experience**: Directional awareness, not implementation depth

### Primary JTBD
Get the AI transition right before the positioning window closes — without wasting budget on vague consulting engagements that deliver decks, not outcomes.

### Trigger Events
*(Hypothesized — needs validation via interviews)*
- Board or investor asks "what's your AI strategy?"
- Competitor launches an AI-powered feature or announces a transformation
- Key employee quits to join an AI-first company ("we're falling behind")
- Internal AI pilot fails or produces no measurable results after 3+ months
- Reading a report like FTI × Last Economy that makes the urgency concrete

### Top Pains
*(Signal from AOB pilot + Malik's practitioner experience)*
- "We've tried AI tools but nobody's using them consistently"
- "I don't know how to tell if an AI implementation is working"
- "We've been burned by consultants who delivered recommendations but no results"
- "I'm worried we're automating the wrong things"

**Confidence**: Medium — based on 1 direct client + inferred from broader advisory patterns

### Desired Outcomes
- A clear AI roadmap they can present to their board
- Measurable evidence of progress (not just "we implemented AI")
- Human team that feels augmented, not threatened
- Positioning advantage before the category matures

### Objections
- "We can't afford a full-time CAO"
- "Is this a real methodology or just a consulting sales pitch?"
- "Will this actually work in our industry?" (especially non-tech industries like wellness)

### Key Vocabulary *(from AOB engagement)*
- "AI adoption" (not "AI transformation" — transformation sounds like disruption)
- "How do I know it's working"
- "What should we automate vs. what should we keep human"
- "I just need someone to tell me what to do"

### How to Reach
- LinkedIn (primary — where they publish and consume business content)
- Podcast appearances (business + leadership-focused)
- Referral from existing clients (high trust path)
- Conference speaking (AI summit, CEO roundtables, industry-specific)

## Persona B: The Technical Leader

### Profile
- **Titles**: Engineering Manager, VP Engineering, CTO (technical founder), Principal Engineer, AI/ML Lead
- **Company size**: Any — this segment spans startup to enterprise
- **Industry**: Software, technical services, AI-native companies
- **AI experience**: Hands-on, building with AI tools daily

### Primary JTBD
Level up their team's agentic engineering practice beyond ad-hoc prompting toward systematic, production-grade AI systems.

### Trigger Events
*(Hypothesized — needs validation via community research)*
- Team is using AI but results are inconsistent and hard to measure
- New AI tools keep appearing and nobody knows what to prioritize
- Building an AI feature from scratch with no framework for how to architect it
- Reading the 11 Levels repo and wanting to assess where they are

### Top Pains
*(Signal from GitHub engagement + technical community patterns)*
- "We're all using AI differently and there's no consistency"
- "I can't evaluate AI tools systematically — they're all marketing"
- "We keep building things that get abandoned because the output quality is unpredictable"
- "I don't know how to assess my team's AI maturity"

**Confidence**: Medium — based on GitHub engagement patterns and community research

### Desired Outcomes
- A shared vocabulary for AI maturity across the team
- A framework to evaluate tools and approaches objectively
- A roadmap from current state to Level [N+2]
- Credibility with non-technical leadership about AI investment

### Key Vocabulary *(from technical communities)*
- "Agentic", "agent loop", "context window", "MCP", "orchestration"
- "In production" (vs. "in a demo")
- "Evaluation framework" / "evals"
- "Hallucination rate", "reliability"

### How to Reach
- GitHub (already there — 11 Levels repo)
- Hacker News
- Reddit: r/MachineLearning, r/LocalLLaMA, r/AIAssistants
- Technical podcasts and YouTube
- Engineering newsletters

## Customer Interview Framework (Q2 2026)

Run 5–8 interviews with advisory prospects or the AOB engagement team.

### Recruiting Message
```
Subject: 30 minutes to help shape Centaurion?

[Name] — I'm doing a short round of conversations to understand how 
leaders like you are thinking about AI transformation. No sales pitch. 
30 minutes, I'll ask questions and listen. You get the unfiltered 
synthesis of what I'm learning across conversations.

Worth it? Reply and I'll send a calendar link.
— Malik
```

### Interview Questions (30 minutes)

**Opening (5 min)**
- "Tell me about your current relationship with AI in your organization."
- "What prompted you to think seriously about AI strategy in the last 6 months?"

**Pain discovery (10 min)**
- "Walk me through the last AI initiative that didn't go the way you expected."
- "What's the most frustrating thing about the AI tools/consultants you've worked with?"
- "When you think about AI 2 years from now — what are you most worried about?"

**Desired outcomes (5 min)**
- "What would 'AI done right' look like for your organization?"
- "How would you know if it was working?"

**Language mining (5 min)**
- "How do you describe your AI situation to your board?"
- "What words do you use when you talk to your team about AI?"

**Alternatives (5 min)**
- "What have you tried before? What worked and what didn't?"
- "Who else were you considering working with?"
