## Top 10 Ideas for Centaurion (Prioritized)

---

### 1. Glossary Marketing (#4 equivalent)
**Why it fits**: Centaurion owns specialized vocabulary nobody else has defined accessibly. Publishing 20–40 glossary pages creates compounding organic traffic with near-zero competition.

**How to start**:
1. Publish 15 seed terms from the product-marketing-context vocabulary list
2. Add `DefinedTerm` + `FAQPage` schema to each
3. Cross-link every instance of each term in blog/methodology pages to its glossary entry

**Expected outcome**: First AI citations and organic traffic within 60–90 days. Long-term: Centaurion becomes the reference definition for these terms.

**Resources**: 2–3 hours per term × 15 terms = ~40 hours total. No budget required.

---

### 2. Conference Speaking (#70)
**Why it fits**: The target buyer (CAO-level, transformation leads) attends AI transformation, CXO, and technical leadership conferences. A 20-minute talk on the Thousand-Day Window or the 11 Levels is a direct pipeline to high-quality inbound.

**How to start**:
1. Build a 20-minute keynote around "The Intelligence Inversion: Why the Thousand-Day Window Closes in 2027"
2. Submit to: Transform (enterprise AI), MLOps World, CXO Summit, AI Summit, local CEO roundtables
3. Publish talk notes as a blog post + LinkedIn article after each event

**Expected outcome**: 1–3 qualified advisory leads per speaking engagement. Brand awareness with enterprise decision-makers.

**Resources**: 8–12 hours to build deck. Travel budget for in-person events (~$500–2,000/event).

---

### 3. LinkedIn Thought Leadership (Audience Building #35)
**Why it fits**: The ICP (mid-market executives, technical leaders, fractional leaders) is densely concentrated on LinkedIn. Malik's existing presence + the framework = strong differentiation in a feed full of generic "AI tips" content.

**How to start**:
1. Establish a weekly publishing cadence: 1 long-form article + 3–4 short posts
2. Content angles: framework illustrations (Three Laws diagrams), controversial takes (most AI consulting is theater), behind-the-scenes (building the exo-cortex), case study snippets (AOB data points)
3. Engage authentically in comments on AI transformation posts from target accounts

**Expected outcome**: 500–2,000 relevant followers in 90 days. Inbound DMs from potential clients.

**Resources**: 3–5 hours/week. No budget required.

---

### 4. GitHub as a Marketing Channel (#133 DevRel)
**Why it fits**: The 11 Levels repo (`MalikJPalamar/centaurion-agentic-engineering-lvl`) is already published. GitHub stars and watchers are a trust signal with technical buyers. Every README is a marketing document.

**How to start**:
1. Upgrade the 11 Levels README: add visual diagram, link to centaurion.me/agentic-engineering, add "Who is Malik Palamar" section with advisory CTA
2. Create a `centaurion-method` repo with the framework as a published document
3. Submit to Awesome Lists (awesome-ai, awesome-agents) for backlinks

**Expected outcome**: Organic GitHub stars, developer-to-leader pipeline, backlinks from curated lists.

**Resources**: 4–6 hours for README improvements. No budget.

### 5. Founder Email List (Founder Emails #47)
**Why it fits**: Personal emails from Malik convert better than brand emails at this stage. A weekly "signal from the field" email (what I'm building, what I'm learning, one prediction error I caught this week) builds a loyal early audience.

**How to start**:
1. Choose platform: Substack (free, built-in discovery) or Resend/Mailchimp (owned list)
2. Name the newsletter: "The Exo-Cortex Brief" or "Prediction Error" — something that owns vocabulary
3. Seed with first 50 subscribers: existing contacts, LinkedIn connections, GitHub stargazers

**Expected outcome**: 200–500 subscribers in 90 days. First advisory clients will come from this list.

**Resources**: 2–3 hours/week. Free to start.

### 6. Podcast Tour (#107)
**Why it fits**: The target buyer consumes business/technology podcasts. Getting Malik on 10–15 relevant podcasts delivers qualified exposure with no ad spend.

**How to start**:
1. Build a one-page pitch: "Malik Palamar, creator of the 11 Levels of Agentic Engineering, on why most AI implementations fail (and what the thermostat problem tells us)"
2. Target: Lenny's Podcast, AI Today, The CXAI Show, Practical AI, Traction, My First Million, Invest Like the Best
3. Start with smaller shows (5k–20k downloads) to build reel, then pitch up

**Expected outcome**: 1–2 qualified leads per episode. Backlinks from show notes. Social proof ("as heard on...").

**Resources**: 1 hour prep per episode. No budget. Lead time: 4–8 weeks to book.

### 7. Original Research / Annual Report (#97)
**Why it fits**: "The State of AI Transformation in Mid-Market Organizations" is a report Centaurion is uniquely positioned to publish. It creates media coverage, backlinks, LinkedIn shares, and lead-gen in one asset.

**How to start**:
1. Design a 10-question survey targeting mid-market executives on AI adoption status, barriers, and measurement
2. Distribute via LinkedIn, newsletter, and partner networks (target 100–200 responses)
3. Publish findings as a PDF lead magnet + key stats as a public web page

**Expected outcome**: 200–500 email captures. Media citations. AI citation in Perplexity/ChatGPT for "AI transformation statistics."

**Resources**: 4–8 hours to design and distribute survey. 8–12 hours to write report. No budget beyond survey tool (Typeform free tier).

---

### 8. Engineering as Marketing — Free Assessment Tool (#15)
**Why it fits**: "What Agentic Engineering Level Are You?" is a self-assessment tool that delivers immediate value, captures leads, and demonstrates the framework in action.

**How to start**:
1. Build a 10-question assessment (can be done with Typeform or simple HTML form)
2. Score respondents → output their current level (1–11) + what's needed to reach the next level
3. Gate the full report (email required); show level on screen immediately

**Expected outcome**: 50–200 assessments/month. 30–50% email capture rate. Strong lead qualifier (someone who takes this is already a warm buyer).

**Resources**: 8–16 hours to build. Free tools or ~$20/month for Typeform.

---

### 9. Reddit Authentic Participation (#38)
**Why it fits**: r/MachineLearning, r/AIAssistants, r/ChatGPT, r/LocalLLaMA are communities where the technical buyer ICP congregates. Thoughtful, non-promotional answers to relevant questions build credibility and get cited by AI.

**How to start**:
1. Set up Reddit account with professional bio (link to GitHub/centaurion.me)
2. Answer 2–3 questions per week on: agentic engineering, AI implementation failures, active inference, exo-cortex setups
3. Never promote directly — provide genuine value, let the profile do the work

**Expected outcome**: Domain authority signals for AI citation. Occasional inbound DMs. Profile views that convert to followers.

**Resources**: 30–60 minutes/week. No budget.

---

### 10. Strategic Comparison Content (#11)
**Why it fits**: "Fractional CAO vs Full-Time CAO" and "Centaurion vs Traditional AI Consulting" are high-intent comparison queries that buyers search at decision stage. Centaurion can own this content with no competition.

**How to start**:
1. Publish 3–5 comparison pages (see `strategy/competitor-alternatives.md` for detailed templates)
2. Target: `/compare/fractional-cao-vs-full-time-cao/`, `/compare/open-loop-vs-closed-loop-ai/`
3. Link from all Work With Us pages

**Expected outcome**: Decision-stage traffic that converts directly to advisory inquiries.

**Resources**: 3–4 hours per page. No budget.
