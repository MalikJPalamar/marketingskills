# Referral Program Strategy — Centaurion.me

**Client:** Centaurion.me (Malik Palamar)
**Phase:** Foundation programs in Phase 1; affiliate and course referral in Phase 2
**Model:** High-value advisory referral — one right introduction is worth more than 100 cold outreach attempts

---

## Overview

Centaurion's market is narrow and relationship-driven. A CEO who has worked with Malik knows three other CEOs running the same organizational problems. A fractional CFO who refers one client to Malik is making a professional reputational bet that Centaurion will deliver.

Referral infrastructure for Centaurion must:
1. Make it easy for satisfied clients and partners to introduce Malik without awkwardness
2. Provide appropriate incentive without reducing the professional nature of the relationship
3. Track where introductions come from and what they produce
4. Scale gracefully from handshake-level tracking (Phase 1) to automated attribution (Phase 2)

The referral program operates in three tracks:
- **Track 1:** Client referrals (existing Pilot/Retainer clients referring peer organizations)
- **Track 2:** Partner referrals (fractional executives and complementary service providers)
- **Track 3:** Course affiliates (Phase 2, when the Centaurion certification course launches)

---

## Track 1: Client Referral Program

### Structure

**Program name:** "The Centaurion Circle" (optional — may simply be informal in Phase 1)

**Mechanism:** After a client completes the Pilot Audit or enters the second month of a retainer, Malik has a direct conversation:

*"If there's an organization in your network that you think would benefit from what we've built together, I'd welcome an introduction. This is how most of my best client relationships start."*

This is not transactional. It is an invitation framed around helping a colleague. The incentive structure below exists but is not the lead — the relationship is.

### Incentive Structure

**Option A — Service credit (preferred):**
- Introduce us to one organization that becomes a Pilot Audit client → receive a $1,000 credit toward the next month of retainer or a future engagement
- Introduce us to one organization that signs a Fractional CAO retainer → receive a $2,000 service credit

**Why service credit, not cash:** Cash referral fees can feel transactional and may create compliance concerns for executives in regulated industries. Service credit reinforces the ongoing relationship and keeps Malik top of mind when the referrer's next challenge arises.

**Option B — Reciprocal introduction:**
For clients who are themselves service providers or advisors, offer a reciprocal introduction within Malik's network rather than a financial incentive. "You introduce me, and I'll introduce you to [relevant contact]." This works especially well in the fractional executive community.

**Option C — Recognition:**
Feature the referring client in "The Prediction Error Brief" (newsletter) as a case study or spotlight — with their permission. This is valuable for clients who want public credibility as AI-forward organizations.

### When to Ask

The timing of the referral ask is critical. Do not ask:
- In the first 30 days of an engagement (relationship not yet established)
- During a difficult period of the engagement (when client is frustrated)
- At contract renewal time (creates impression that referral is tied to renewal)

The right moment to ask:
- At the end of Pilot Audit delivery, when the client has seen the diagnosis and expresses satisfaction
- At the 60-day mark of a retainer, after a clear win or milestone has been achieved
- When a client spontaneously says "you should talk to my friend at X company" — this is the organic prompt; follow up with the formal offer

### Referral Process for Clients

Provide clients with a simple introduction template they can forward:

```
Subject: Introduction — Malik Palamar, Centaurion.me

[Peer's name],

I've been working with Malik Palamar at Centaurion.me on our AI augmentation architecture for the past [X months]. He works with a specific methodology — the Centaurion Method — that's different from what you get from a typical consulting engagement. Very diagnostic, very structured.

Given what you've described about your [AI integration challenges / team structure / automation backlog], I think a conversation with Malik would be worth 30 minutes.

I'll let you two connect from here.

Malik's contact: [email or Calendly link]

— [Client name]
```

The key: the template is short, doesn't oversell, and positions the introduction as a peer recommendation, not a referral for compensation.

### Tracking Client Referrals (Phase 1)

In Phase 1, tracking is manual:

**Referral log (spreadsheet or CRM):**
```
Referring client: [Name]
Referred contact: [Name, Company, Title]
Introduction date: [Date]
Method: [Email intro / LinkedIn / Event]
First contact with Malik: [Date]
Outcome: Discovery call booked / No response / Not a fit / Proposal sent / Closed
Service credit issued: [Amount, Date]
Notes: [Context on the relationship]
```

This log serves two purposes: tracking attribution and identifying which clients are strong referral sources (to invest more in those relationships).

## Track 2: Partner Referral Program

### The Partner Ecosystem

Centaurion's target clients need more than AI augmentation advisory. They also need fractional CFOs, CMOs, CPOs, and other executives. These fractional service providers are natural referral partners because:

1. They serve the same ICP (mid-market organizations sub-$50M)
2. They have client relationships where AI transformation is surfacing as a challenge
3. They are not competitive with Centaurion
4. They respect referral reciprocity as a professional norm

### Partner Types

**Tier 1 — High-quality referral partners:**
- Fractional CFOs (often the first to see where AI is a cost or efficiency problem)
- Fractional CMOs (AI-powered marketing stack, content operations)
- Fractional CPOs (product development with AI components)
- Venture advisors (portfolio companies needing AI infrastructure)
- PE-backed portfolio company advisors

**Tier 2 — Domain-adjacent partners:**
- Technology consultants / implementation shops (they build, Malik advises — complementary)
- Legal/compliance professionals in AI-adjacent industries
- Executive coaches to C-suite (they have access, Centaurion has solution)

**Tier 3 — Community/network partners:**
- YPO chapters and EO (Entrepreneurs' Organization) chapters
- CEO peer groups and Vistage-type organizations
- Industry associations in target verticals

### Partner Referral Incentive

**Standard partner referral fee:**
- $500 for every introduced Pilot Audit that closes
- $1,000 for every Fractional CAO retainer that initiates from a partner introduction

**Payment timing:** 30 days after engagement commencement and first invoice paid

**Alternative:** Revenue share on first 3 months of retainer (10–15%) — more compelling for partners who are confident in client quality

**Note on IRS compliance:** Any referral fees paid to individuals or entities require a W-9 before payment and are 1099-reportable if > $600/year. Use a simple contract (one-pager) to formalize partner relationships.

### Partner Agreement Template

Keep it simple — one page, plain language:

```
Centaurion.me Partner Referral Agreement

Partner: [Name / Entity]
Date: [Date]

1. Referrals: Partner may introduce Malik Palamar (Centaurion.me) to potential clients.
2. Qualified referral: An introduction that results in a signed engagement agreement.
3. Referral fee: $500 per Pilot Audit (30-day), $1,000 per retainer engagement, paid 30 days after first invoice.
4. Non-exclusivity: This agreement is non-exclusive. Both parties may engage with third parties freely.
5. Confidentiality: Both parties agree to maintain client confidentiality.
6. Term: Ongoing until either party terminates with 30 days written notice.

Signed: [Malik Palamar] [Partner name] Date: [Date]
```

### Recruiting Partners

**Phase 1 approach:**
1. Identify 10–15 target fractional executives in Malik's existing network
2. Send a direct outreach (not a form or automated sequence) explaining the referral structure
3. Schedule a 20-minute "partner sync" call to understand each other's client base
4. Follow up with a one-page overview of Centaurion ideal clients

**Partner outreach message (LinkedIn DM or email):**

```
[Name],

I've been building out a formal partner program for Centaurion.me — I help mid-market organizations architect their human-AI augmentation infrastructure. Fractional [CFO/CMO/CPO] work often surfaces the same problem I solve, just from a different angle.

If you're open to a 20-minute call to see if there's a mutual referral fit, I'd like to compare notes on our client profiles.

— Malik
```

### Partner Communication Cadence

- **Quarterly:** Email update summarizing recent client wins, new service offerings, any changes to ICP
- **Monthly:** Share one relevant resource (newsletter issue, case study, framework) — keeps Centaurion top of mind
- **Ad hoc:** Personal notes when a client outcome is relevant to a partner's domain

Do not spam partners. One high-signal touchpoint per month is better than five generic check-ins.

## Track 3: Course Affiliate Program (Phase 2)

### Context

When the Centaurion certification course launches (Phase 2, expected Q3–Q4 2026), a formal affiliate program becomes viable. Course affiliates differ from advisory referral partners: they can promote at scale via their audiences (newsletters, LinkedIn followings, YouTube channels).

### Affiliate Structure

**Commission:** 30% of course revenue per sale (industry standard for digital courses at this price point)

**Attribution window:** 30-day cookie

**Payout:** Monthly, via Stripe or PayPal, minimum $100 threshold

**Platform recommendation:** Use Rewardful (integrates with Stripe) or ThriveCart (handles both checkout and affiliate management) — see `tools/integrations/rewardful.md` for setup details

### Affiliate Tiers

| Tier | Condition | Commission |
|---|---|---|
| Standard Affiliate | Any approved affiliate | 30% |
| Partner Affiliate | Fractional executive referral partners (Track 2) | 35% |
| Founding Affiliate | First 10 affiliates who generate > 5 sales | 40% lifetime |

### Affiliate Recruitment (Phase 2)

**Priority targets:**
- AI/ML educators with LinkedIn audiences > 5,000 (adjacent technical content)
- Fractional executive thought leaders (they influence Centaurion's ICP)
- Productivity/future-of-work newsletter writers
- GitHub educators with engineering audiences
- Technical founders who write publicly about AI architecture

**Affiliate onboarding kit:**
1. Affiliate access credentials and referral link
2. One-page summary: "Who is the Centaurion course for?"
3. 3 pre-written LinkedIn posts they can customize
4. Email copy for their newsletter
5. UTM-tagged referral links for each channel type
6. Monthly affiliate newsletter with conversion stats and new assets

## Attribution and Tracking

### Phase 1 — Manual Tracking

Use a shared Notion page or Google Sheet:

```
Column headers:
- Referral track (Client / Partner / Organic)
- Referring source (name or organization)
- Referred contact (name, title, company)
- Date of introduction
- First meeting date
- Outcome (In process / No fit / Proposal / Closed)
- Deal value
- Referral fee / credit status
- Notes
```

Review this log monthly. It will reveal:
- Which partners send the highest-quality referrals
- Whether client referrals convert better than partner referrals
- Average time from introduction to close (sets expectation for partners)

### Phase 2 — CRM-Based Tracking

When a CRM is implemented (see revops.md), migrate referral attribution into contact source fields:
- `Lead source: Referral`
- `Referral source: [Name]`
- `Referral type: Client / Partner / Affiliate`

Use this data to calculate partner-level LTV and adjust incentive structures accordingly.

### Key Metrics to Monitor

| Metric | Definition | Phase 1 Target |
|---|---|---|
| Referral introduction rate | % of active clients who make at least one introduction | > 30% within 90 days of engagement |
| Referral close rate | % of introductions that result in a closed engagement | > 25% |
| Partner referral volume | Number of qualified introductions per partner per quarter | 1–3 per active partner |
| Referral CAC | Total referral incentives paid / referral-sourced revenue | < 8% |

## Program Principles

1. **Relationships before mechanics.** The referral program formalizes what should already be happening — satisfied clients and partners making introductions because they believe in the work. The incentive structure supports this; it does not replace it.

2. **Quality over volume.** One introduction to the right CEO is worth more than 20 introductions to contacts who don't fit the ICP. Track referral quality, not just referral quantity.

3. **Make it easy.** Provide templates, referral links, and talking points so the barrier to introduction is near zero. A partner who wants to introduce Malik should never have to think hard about how.

4. **Close the loop.** Always update the referring client or partner on the outcome of an introduction. "We had a great conversation — not the right timing for them right now, but I appreciated the intro." This builds trust and encourages future referrals.

5. **Referral programs compound.** A well-run referral program gets better over time as the network of satisfied clients and partners grows. Invest in it from Day 1, even if it feels premature.
